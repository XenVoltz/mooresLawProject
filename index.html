Who is Gordon Moore?

Gordon Moore is an American engineer who co-founded Intel. He studied chemistry and physics, eventually receiving a Ph.D. from the California Institute of Technology. Later on, after graduating, Gordon Moore joined the Applied Physics Laboratory at Johns Hopkins University. There, he studied the physical chemistry of solid rocket propellants used by the United States Navy. After a while, he returned to Califiornia to work at Shcokely Semiconductor Labratory. After about a year-and-a-half, Moore had lost appeal and decided to resign, along with seven other colleagues. He went on to join with Fairchild Camera and Instrument Corporation. They eventually formed a new company called Fairchild Semiconductor Corporation. In 1968, Moore and Noyce left Fairchilid to form Intel Corporation in Santa Clara.
