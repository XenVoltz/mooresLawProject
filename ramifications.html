Ramifications, Industry Response and Timeline.

Because Moore’s law expected growth to be so rapid, there is the problem of obsolescence. Technology is growing so fast that computers, phones and other technology become old and outdated very fast. When smart phones started becoming popular, you could keep a phone going strong for a year or two at most before the next phone was insanely faster and more efficient. Now we have so much technology waste that we had to find ways to properly dispose of it. That isn’t completely a bad thing, though, as it created many jobs. I worked at a warehouse for four years that helped repurpose and recycle old technology. So it did create jobs, but we still have a huge amount of waste in landfills thanks to how fast technology has grown. This also has an effect on some businesses that require constant uptime of their systems. As their systems become outdated, these companies are sometimes forced to stay on old systems and software because they can’t afford any down time due to updates that may break their systems.
